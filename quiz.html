The State Of Tennessee's 5th Grade Students Evaluation Test

Methodology and Information Regarding The Test

Tennessee highly values each and every one of our students education. As of recently, we have taken a more statistical approach and would like to ensure all of our students recieve a quality education. To ensure this, we have created the following test, which each completion will be recorded. We will use this data to modify our current curriculum, in order to approach the strengths and weaknesses of our 5th grade student population as a unit. The Tennessee Department Of Education strongly supports the success of our future generations, so participation is mandated for all schools who recieve financial aid from the state.

The Test is over the following topics:

- Math

- Science

- English

- History


The following test contains eight questions, with two coming from each of the listed categories. You have one attempt at each question, and each question is auto-graded. The questions are derived from the currently implemented curriculum.

Going

About

This test was created as a result of the state falling below the median of all states within the United States. With the negative feedback recieved from our prior implemented Common Core, we decided to obtain all of the data we could recieve, in order to create a more directed and targeted system of education. With the recent bill passed by Govenor Bill Lee, we are extremely motivated to create a curriculum that is better suited for our current population. Within the next ten years, we will use this data to ensure that our education is within the top of the country, to ensure a better future for our children. We chose the four categories based on what we believed to be the most important for a fifth grade student to learn, but we plan to implement more catagories and questions based on the feedback we recieve.
